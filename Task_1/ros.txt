Based on my simple understanding, ROS (Robot Operating System) isn't a programming language. It's more of a framework or a bridge that connects different parts of a robot (like sensors and motors) and lets them talk to each other.As someone from a mechanical background, I have a clear, physical imagination of how movements and reactions should happen in any mechanism. For me, ROS is the system that translates this mechanical imagination into software commands that actually run on the hardware.Its main idea is to make controlling a robot much easier. It gives us ready-to-use tools that make the mechanics and the software work together as one system, without needing to write complex code from scratch for every single part of the robot.
